Day in the life of a social worker: Jackie

Context

This video hopes to showcase the day in the life of a social worker in the childsafety department.
With the goals to not only showcase how the department is structure, how restortive practice working with Indigenous children is implemented,
but also an inner look of how working for a non-profit childwelfare agency is like.
I also initiated an interview with Jackie so the viewers can have a better understanding of the history of the social worker, Jackie, in the video.
You can visit the blog published on the VACFSS website here.

My Role

As the PR and Communciations Assistant, I worked with the Associate to film, edit, and coordinate the approval process when my supervisor left the agency.

Iterations

The inital verison was over 10mins, and after weeks of editing, I was finally able to cut the video down to 6 mins. There was also use of the wording caseload, which was replaced by later recording of the voice over to use families, as social workers work with people and is a more appropiate term in this case.

Audio Re-recording

It was my first time working with a camera and a gimbal, so the shoots did not turn as the best.
There were also recording of audio as the wording had to be changes in certain parts.
Luckly, it was in a scene where Jackie was looking away from the camera.

Reflection

As an adminstrative worker, and non-Indigenous person, I needed to be careful in filming,
and later editing to inlude/protray the day inthe life of a social worker as honestly and factually as possible.
Hence the video was completed in 2023, but took another 3 months of approval from the directors,
and CEO to be released on the offical website and Youtube Channel.
Even my reflection which I published on Linkedin took me days to rewrite,with emphsis that the video cannot represent every social workers's experience.
As of right now the video has gained 170 views, which is not a lot but considering how the videos created by a professional team only gathered the same amount of views,
and had led our website 700+ traffic visits, I would consider this a minor success.
