My Role

I am the Public Relations and Communciations, part of my duties includes creating designs. The Winter Solstice greeting cards are part of external communcation and public relations.
The design is created my me and the overview of the supervisor, and then approved by directors, and finally the CEO for approval before printing.

Project Purpose

The purpose of the card is to send our gratitude for the collaboration and the support they have provided us through the year on this day triditionally celebrated by the Indigenous Peoples.
Each of the 3 locations at VACFSS also recieves different volume of cards which needs coordinating.
I also part of that planning process with support from my supervisor.
The printed greeting cards will be sent out to our 250+ community partners and ~100 caregivers.

Process

Insperation

Since the beginning of my position in May 2020, I developed a habit to collect artworks I love, and are created by Indigenous artist in the lowermain land.
I add those works to the side of my workplace along with digital archives to support the creation of the graphic design and illustrations I do for VACFSS.

Sketching

I find drawing down ideas to the best practice, so for the first few weeks,
I educated myself on Indigenous methology, cutlural terms, and symbolism of various nations of the Coast Salish and Vancouver area.

Illustration verses hand drawn

One of the challenges I faced was how the painting on an ipad lacked scalability and the ability to adjest colors.
Where as on Illustrator I could sketch out concepts with vector and the lines would never be blurry.
-Coast Salish works, and formline design also conposed on clean and smooth outlines, and symobles which I felt my drawings lacked.
So dispite having worked on paintng and sketching on Procreate for many days, I went on to vectorize my design on illustrator.

Editing

I recieved critique from my suprervisor at the time that the raven was a little distracting, and not "friendly enough" so I had to remove in the next stage of my process.

Indigenous Mythology

However, having purchesed books on Indigenous stories, and learned so much. I felt the need to include them in my piece.
Instead of people that are the same only with different colors like in the inital painting.
Each figure would be a spirit of such as the thunderbird, the men in the woods,the Eagle,and the raven dancing around the fire.

Thunderbird

And animal with great power, related to it's name, thunder.

Raven

The trickster, the raven is smart but also the truble maker.

Men in the woods

The lost man in the wood, a story told like the bigfoot.

Eagle

A big with pride and intellgence.

Final Illustration editing process

The final illustration went through at least 6 arounds of major editing, each time I printed a design and noticed details to be changes.
My Goal was for the viewer to feel as though they are approching a ceremony in the woods, under the gazes of the sun and the moon,
deep in the forest and before the waters and the distant mountains.
